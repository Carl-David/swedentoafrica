The true size of Africa

2014-02-27

When looking at a traditional map, it might seem like I’m already halfway down to Cape Town. But 1/3 is probably more accurate, and this “optical illusion” is caused by something called map projection. Not surprisingly, visualizing a spherical planet on a flat surface is not trivial. Traditional maps use the Mercator projection that causes land mass close to the equator to look smaller than it actually is, and vice versa. That’s for example why Greenland looks as big as Africa, when in fact Africa is fourteen times larger!
